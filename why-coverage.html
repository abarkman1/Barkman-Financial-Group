Medicare
Robert, 68
Retired · Medicare beneficiary

Robert enrolled in Medicare Part A and B when he turned 65 — but no one told him those alone don't cover everything. After a brief hospital stay and outpatient surgery, he received $4,200 in bills Medicare didn't cover. He didn't know Medigap plans exist to cover exactly these gaps.

The difference coverage makes
With a Medicare Supplement plan, Robert's out-of-pocket cost would have been $0. A plan would have cost him $115/month — far less than one unexpected bill.

Kevin kept meaning to get life insurance but thought he was too young to worry about it. When he passed unexpectedly from a heart attack, his wife was left with a mortgage, two kids in school, and no financial safety net. What would have cost him $40/month became an insurmountable gap.

The Hard Truth

The best time to get coverage
was yesterday. The second best
time is today.

Every day you wait, coverage becomes either harder to qualify for or more expensive. Health changes. Age increases premiums. Waiting costs money — and sometimes costs everything.

Age affects your rate
Life insurance premiums increase approximately 8–10% for every year you wait. A policy at 55 costs significantly less than the same policy at 62.
Health changes lock you out
A new diagnosis can make you uninsurable for many plans. Applying while you're healthy gives you the most options at the best prices.
Medicare windows are strict
Miss your Medicare enrollment window and you face lifetime premium penalties. There are specific dates you must know.
Your family can't wait
Coverage only works if it's in place before something happens. The people who need it most are the ones who waited.
